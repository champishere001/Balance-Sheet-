Why PLFS was Revamped in 2025

Primary Objective: Enable higher-frequency labour force indicators with improved accuracy and granularity
  • A rotational panel sampling design will be used in the survey.
  • The rotational scheme will be of two years duration to accommodate the changes in the frame in the intercensal period
  • Each selected household will be visited four times in four consecutive months—one with first visit schedule and other three with the revisit schedule. [cite: 89]
  • Monthly estimates will be generated for successive months without any break in the series starting from the fourth month, ensuring a 75% matching between two consecutive months. [cite: 90]
Source: MoSPI Changes in PLFS Document (2025) | Effective: January 2025 onwards

Schedule 0.0PL – Second Stage Strata (SSS)

Basis: Members with general education ≥ Secondary level (10th standard pass and above)

Rural Areas (3 SSS)

SSS Criteria HH Selected
SSS 1 2+ educated members 4 hh
SSS 2 Exactly 1 educated 6 hh
SSS 3 0 educated 2 hh

Urban Areas (4 SSS)

SSS Criteria HH Selected
SSS 1 3+ educated 4 hh
SSS 2 Exactly 2 educated 3 hh
SSS 3 Exactly 1 educated 3 hh
SSS 4 0 educated 2 hh
Total: 12 households selected per FSU | Objective: Ensure adequate representation of educated households in labour force estimates

Schedule 0.0PL – Household Selection Process

Step-by-Step Selection Procedure

1
Complete Listing: Enumerate ALL households in selected SU/FSU without omission
2
Education Count: For each household, count members with secondary education (≥10th standard)
3
SSS Formation: Group households into respective SSS categories based on education count
4
Cumulative Totals: Prepare cumulative list of households within each SSS
5
Random Selection: Apply systematic random sampling using prescribed random numbers
6
Final Sample: Select specified number of households from each SSS (total 12 hh)
Quality Check: Verify that selected households are geographically distributed across the FSU to avoid clustering bias
